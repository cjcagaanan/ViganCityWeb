About Vigan

Vigan City, located in Ilocos Sur, Philippines, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture. It is famous for Calle Crisologo, a historic cobblestone street lined with ancestral houses, antique shops, and horse-drawn carriages called kalesas. The city showcases a blend of Filipino and Spanish influences, evident in its culture, cuisine, and traditions. Tourists can explore landmarks such as the Bantay Bell Tower, Plaza Salcedo, and the Vigan Cathedral, or enjoy local delicacies like empanada and bagnet. With its rich history and vibrant heritage, Vigan remains one of the most charming and culturally significant destinations in the Philippines.

Top Attractions

Vigan City is home to many historical and cultural landmarks that reflect its rich heritage. From well-preserved Spanish-era streets to iconic religious sites and interactive museums, each attraction offers a glimpse into the city's vibrant past. Whether you're strolling along Calle Crisologo, exploring historical landmarks, or indulging in local delicacies, Vigan promises an unforgettable experience.

Calle Crisologo

Calle Crisologo is Vigan’s most famous and picturesque street, featuring cobblestone pathways lined with ancestral houses that showcase Spanish-era architecture. The street is free from modern vehicles, allowing visitors to take a leisurely stroll or ride a traditional kalesa (horse-drawn carriage) while admiring antique shops, souvenir stores, and charming cafés. Walking along this historic road is like stepping back in time to the colonial period.

Vigan Cathedral

Also known as the Saint Paul Metropolitan Cathedral, the Vigan Cathedral is one of the most significant religious landmarks in the city. Built in the 1600s, the cathedral features Baroque-style architecture with thick buttresses to withstand earthquakes. It serves as the seat of the Archdiocese of Nueva Segovia and remains a place of worship for many locals and visitors alike. The grand structure, with its impressive façade and serene interior, is a must-visit for those interested in history and spirituality.

Bantay Church Bell Tower

Perched on a hill overlooking Vigan, the Bantay Church Bell Tower offers breathtaking views of the city and its surrounding landscapes. Built in 1591, the bell tower once served as a watchtower to guard against potential invasions. Today, it stands as an iconic symbol of Vigan’s resilience and a popular spot for tourists seeking scenic panoramas and historical insights. Climbing to the top provides a rewarding experience with a spectacular vantage point.

Crisologo Museum

The Crisologo Museum is a well-preserved ancestral home-turned-museum that offers a glimpse into the life of Congressman Floro S. Crisologo, a prominent political figure assassinated in Vigan. The museum showcases family heirlooms, antique furniture, and historical artifacts that reflect the political climate of the past. Visitors can explore the exhibits and learn more about the contributions of the Crisologo family to Ilocano history.

Pagburnayan Jar Factory

The Pagburnayan Jar Factory preserves Vigan’s traditional pottery-making techniques, passed down through generations. Here, skilled artisans shape clay into burnay jars using manual methods, demonstrating the craftsmanship of this centuries-old tradition. Visitors can watch the pottery-making process up close and even try molding their own jars. These earthenware jars, once used for storing water, vinegar, and basi (sugarcane wine), remain an important part of Vigan’s heritage.

Plaza Burgos

Named in honor of Padre Jose Burgos, one of the martyred priests of the Gomburza trio, Plaza Burgos serves as a vibrant gathering place in Vigan. The plaza is known for its street food stalls, where visitors can try the famous Vigan empanada and okoy (shrimp fritters). Located near key historical sites, Plaza Burgos is a perfect spot to relax, take photos, and immerse in the local culture.

Baluarte Zoo

Owned by former governor Chavit Singson, Baluarte Zoo spans approximately 100 hectares and features a wide variety of animals, including tigers, deer, parrots, and reptiles. The zoo offers interactive experiences, allowing visitors to feed and interact with some of the animals. With its open spaces, safari-like enclosures, and free admission, Baluarte Zoo is a great place for families and animal lovers to explore.

Dancing Fountain Show at Plaza Salcedo

Every evening, Plaza Salcedo comes to life with a mesmerizing Dancing Fountain Show, featuring synchronized water jets, colorful lights, and lively music. This free attraction provides entertainment for both tourists and locals, offering a magical display that rivals fountain shows in other countries. Whether you're visiting during the day to admire the historical surroundings or at night for the dazzling water spectacle, Plaza Salcedo remains a must-see spot in Vigan.

Events

Vigan City Fiesta and Longganisa Festival

Annually in late January, with the main events typically culminating on January 22.

This festival honors St. Paul the Apostle, Vigan's patron saint, and celebrates the city's rich history and its renowned longganisa. The 2025 festivities are expected to follow a similar schedule, with events leading up to January 22.

Viva Vigan Binatbatan Festival of the Arts

First week of May.

This festival celebrates Vigan's heritage, particularly its weaving industry, and aims to preserve and promote Ilocano culture.

Tres de Mayo Festival

May 3

A religious and cultural celebration in honor of Apo Lakay (Sto. Cristo Milagroso) at Simbaan a Bassit.

Raniag Vigan Twilight Festival

Late October to early November

A festival of lights with lantern parades, candle float processions, and Halloween-themed events.

Vigan Christmas Fantasy

Entire month of December

Features a grand lighting ceremony, Christmas bazaar, fireworks, and the "Pailaw sa Vigan" light show.
